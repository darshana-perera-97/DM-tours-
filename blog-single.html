The Sacred Temple of the Tooth Relic: A Spiritual Journey in Kandy

March 15, 2025
By Sarah Johnson
8 min read
1,247 views

Discover the spiritual heart of Sri Lanka at the Temple of the Tooth Relic in Kandy. This UNESCO World Heritage site houses one of Buddhism's most sacred relics and offers visitors a profound glimpse into the country's rich religious heritage.

The History of the Sacred Tooth Relic

The Temple of the Tooth Relic, also known as Sri Dalada Maligawa, is one of the most sacred Buddhist temples in the world. According to legend, after the Buddha's cremation in 543 BCE, four teeth were retrieved from the ashes. One of these sacred teeth was brought to Sri Lanka in the 4th century CE, hidden in the hair of Princess Hemamali and her husband Prince Dantha.

The temple was originally built by King Vimaladharmasuriya I in 1595, and has since been expanded and renovated by successive kings. The current structure dates back to the 18th century, during the reign of King Kirti Sri Rajasinha.

Architectural Marvel of the Temple

The temple complex is a stunning example of traditional Kandyan architecture, featuring intricate wood carvings, golden roofs, and elaborate frescoes. The main shrine room, where the sacred tooth relic is housed, is located on the upper floor and is accessible only during specific puja (worship) times.

The temple's design incorporates elements of both Buddhist and Hindu architecture, reflecting the cultural diversity of Sri Lanka. The golden roof, known as the "golden canopy," is a distinctive feature that can be seen from miles around.

Daily Rituals and Ceremonies

Three times daily, the sacred tooth relic is ceremonially displayed to devotees during elaborate puja ceremonies. These ceremonies, accompanied by traditional drumming and chanting, create an atmosphere of deep spirituality and reverence.

The most important ceremony is the evening puja, which includes the ritual bathing of the relic in a special herbal preparation. This ceremony is attended by hundreds of devotees and tourists alike, creating a truly memorable experience.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit the Temple of the Tooth Relic is during the evening puja ceremony, which typically takes place around 6:30 PM. This allows visitors to experience both the architectural beauty of the temple and the spiritual atmosphere of the ceremony.

It's also worth visiting during the annual Esala Perahera festival in July/August, when the sacred tooth relic is paraded through the streets of Kandy in a grand procession featuring elephants, dancers, and musicians.

Practical Information for Visitors

  • Opening Hours: 5:30 AM - 8:00 PM daily
  • Puja Times: 5:30 AM, 9:30 AM, 6:30 PM
  • Dress Code: Modest clothing covering shoulders and knees
  • Photography: Not allowed inside the main shrine room
  • Entrance Fee: Free for locals, small fee for foreigners

Nearby Attractions

While in Kandy, make sure to visit other notable attractions such as the Royal Botanical Gardens, the Kandy Lake, and the Kandyan Art Association. The city also offers excellent opportunities for shopping traditional handicrafts and sampling local cuisine.

Conclusion

A visit to the Temple of the Tooth Relic is more than just a tourist attraction â€“ it's a spiritual journey that connects visitors to the heart of Sri Lankan culture and Buddhism. The temple's serene atmosphere, rich history, and architectural beauty make it a must-visit destination for anyone traveling to Sri Lanka.

Whether you're a spiritual seeker, a history enthusiast, or simply curious about different cultures, the Temple of the Tooth Relic offers an unforgettable experience that will leave you with a deeper understanding of Sri Lanka's cultural heritage.
